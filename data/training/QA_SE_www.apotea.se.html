User guidance for medicinal products containing diclofenac for external use

Use with reflection

Diclofenac negatively affects the environment, used with thought.

Instruction
  1. Apply the gel as directed.
  2. Wipe the leftover gel from your hands with an absorbent paper (e.g. kitchen paper) or wet wipe and then wash your hands to avoid contact with your mouth and eyes, provided, of course, that it is not the hands to be treated. The paper or wipe should be thrown into the trash after use.
  3. Do not use tightly seated dressings where the gel has been applied.
  4. Avoid exposure to sunlight in the treated area and up to 2 weeks after stopping treatment.
  5. The medicine container must be submitted in pharmacies where it is sorted as medicinal material waste. This applies regardless of whether the container is out of stock or not.
Warnings and precautions

Diclofenac should not be used by pregnant women during the last three months of pregnancy. Use of diclofenac in pregnancy and breast-feeding should always be done on a doctor's prescription.
